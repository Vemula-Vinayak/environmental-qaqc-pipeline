# Technical Summary — Environmental Data QA/QC and Regulatory Compliance Review

**Project type:** Data validation & regulatory compliance pipeline (synthetic data, modeled on real EDD formats and EPA standards)
**Records reviewed:** 2,400 groundwater analytical results across 5 sites / 38 monitoring locations
**Period:** January 2023 – June 2025

## 1. Purpose

This deliverable summarizes the results of an automated data validation (QA/QC) and
regulatory compliance review performed on laboratory analytical data submitted in an
EarthSoft EQuIS-style Electronic Data Deliverable (EDD) format. The review was performed
to identify data quality issues prior to database loading and to flag analytical results
requiring regulatory follow-up, consistent with standard environmental consulting data
management workflows.

## 2. Data Validation Results

| Check Category | Records Reviewed | Pass Rate | Fail Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Required Field Completeness | 2,400 | 97.96% | 2.04% |
| Duplicate Sample ID | 2,400 | 98.08% | 1.92% |
| Physically Possible Value Range | 2,400 | 98.17% | 1.83% |
| Holding Time Compliance | 2,400 | 88.71% | 11.29% |
| Regulatory Threshold (MCL) Compliance | 2,400 | 84.92% | 15.08% |

**Overall record-level QA/QC pass rate: 84.3%.**

Holding time compliance was the largest data-quality issue category (11.29% violation
rate), concentrated in volatile organic compound (VOC) results, which carry the
shortest EPA-standard holding time (14 days) and are therefore most sensitive to
lab turnaround or sample shipment delays. These records are flagged for laboratory
follow-up and, where holding times were exceeded, results should be qualified or
reported with a data-usability caveat in the final regulatory submission per standard
data validation practice.

Required-field, duplicate-ID, and impossible-value issues were each under 2.1% and are
typical of routine EDD ingestion error rates; all flagged records were isolated for
correction with the originating laboratory before being loaded into the production
database.

## 3. Regulatory Compliance Review

Each analytical result with an applicable EPA Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) was
compared against that threshold. 362 of 2,400 comparable results (15.08%) exceeded
their regulatory MCL.

**Top exceedance hotspots (by site/analyte):**

| Site | Analyte | Samples | Exceedances | Exceedance Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Industrial Park – Elizabeth | 1,1-Dichloroethylene | 48 | 14 | 29.2% |
| Former Dry Cleaner – Newark | Cadmium | 35 | 10 | 28.6% |
| Chemical Plant – Linden | Mercury | 39 | 11 | 28.2% |
| Former Gas Station – Paterson | Cadmium | 32 | 9 | 28.1% |
| Former Dry Cleaner – Newark | Tetrachloroethylene | 25 | 7 | 28.0% |

VOC exceedances (TCE, PCE, benzene) at the former dry cleaner and gas station sites are
consistent with the known historical use at those property types and would be flagged
as priority locations for continued monitoring or remediation evaluation under a
standard investigation/remediation program.

## 4. Geographic Distribution

An interactive map of all 38 monitoring locations, color-coded by validation/exceedance
status, is provided (`gis_sample_locations_map.html`) to support coordination with GIS
staff and project teams reviewing spatial exceedance patterns across the five sites.

## 5. Recommendations

1. Route the 271 holding-time-flagged records back to the laboratory data validator for
   qualification before regulatory submission.
2. Prioritize follow-up monitoring at the five hotspot site/analyte combinations identified above.
3. Resolve the 49 missing-field and 46 duplicate-ID records with the lab prior to EQuIS load.
4. Continue trend monitoring on the quarterly exceedance-rate chart to track remediation progress over time.

*This summary, the validation dataset, and supporting figures were generated by an
automated QA/QC pipeline built in Python (pandas/NumPy) that replicates the manual data
review process typically performed in EQuIS prior to client deliverable and regulatory
submission preparation.*
